Photography Collection 001

City Stacks

This photograph was captured in Toronto, inspired by the contrast between dense urban architecture and the soft, overcast sky. I was drawn to the layered textures of the buildings and the way they create a sense of rhythm and repetition within the cityscape. The composition reflects my eye for structure and balance through the use of strong vertical lines and varying building heights. The placement of the buildings naturally guides the viewer’s eye across the frame, while the CN Tower is subtly framed in the background, acting as a focal point without overpowering the scene. The repetition of geometric forms, combined with negative space in the sky, creates depth and visual clarity. This piece highlights my ability to find cohesion in complex environments, using composition to transform a busy urban setting into a structured and visually engaging image.
